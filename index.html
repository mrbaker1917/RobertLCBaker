On July 1, 2024, Robert L. C. Baker passed away. He was 90 years old.

He loved Canada, so maybe it is fitting that he died on Canada Day.

Bob was a good, kind-hearted person, friendly, curious, and loving. He loved life very much, and that is probably why he struggled so much to let it go.

Robert was born in Vulcan, but raised in Coaldale, Alberta, and became a social studies teacher after graduating from the University of Alberta. He soon became a vice-principal, and then for many years a principal at several schools in the Edmonton Public School System. He retired at the young age of 55 (1988). But he did not really stop working then. He helped fund raise for the building of a performing arts centre in Sherwood Park, Festival Place, and then became a school board trustee in the Elk Island School District. Along with my mom Loretta, he also opened a bed and breakfast in Sherwood Park, before finally really retiring in the mid-1990s. He and Loretta then set about travelling all over the world, from Bakersfield, California, to Istanbul, to Mexico, Cuba, Holland, the UK, and then cruises across Europe, including a brief visit to St. Petersburg, and the Baltic States. They also spent some winters in Maui, Hawaii, and Mesa, Arizona, and took their last cruise through the Panama Canal in 2017. Dad lived a long life, full of many joyful moments and sad times too. He was, to me at least, a great listener, and always wanting to help, especially to help his children, but others as well. He loved to laugh, to tell stories, to talk and joke around with one and all. He made a deliberate and long-standing effort always to tell a caller or visitor how wonderful it was to hear from them. I take from this habit a desire always to make others know how much he appreciated them as human beings. I cannot imagine how I would have got along without him. I will miss him so.
