On July 1, 2024, Robert L. C. Baker passed away. He was 90 years old.

He loved Canada, so maybe it is fitting that he died on Canada Day.

Bob was a good, kind-hearted person, friendly, curious, and loving. He loved life very much, and that is probably why he struggled so much to let it go.

Robert is survived by his daughter Carol Elizabeth Baker, his two sons, Mark Baker and Tim Baker, as well as his nine grandchildren: Evan, Jillian, and Brandon, Elyse and Samantha, Lily and Jasper, Willow and Georgia.

Robert was born on December 26, 1933, in Vulcan, Alberta, but raised in Coaldale, Alberta. He became a social studies teacher after graduating from the University of Alberta, from which he eventually also earned a Masters degree. He soon became a vice-principal, and then for many years a principal at several schools in the Edmonton Public School System.

In 1956, he met the love of his life, Loretta May Hoffos, and they were married on August 10, 1957. They had five children from 1960 (Carol Elizabeth) to 1971 (Daniel), with three other boys in between (Phil, Mark, and Tim). They lived in Edmonton, Alberta, for about 15 years, and then in Sherwood Park, Alberta, from 1972 until 2017. Loretta worked as a music teacher at Mills Haven Elementary School for many years, and then as the Fine Arts Consultant for the Elk Island School District.

Robert retired at the young age of 55, in 1989. But he did not really stop working then. He helped fund raise for the building of a performing arts centre in Sherwood Park, Festival Place, and then became a school board trustee in the Elk Island School District. He and Loretta also opened a bed and breakfast in Sherwood Park, before finally really retiring in the mid-1990s. They then set about travelling all over the world, from Bakersfield, California, to Istanbul, Mexico, Cuba, Holland, the UK, and then cruises across Europe, including a brief visit to St. Petersburg, and the Baltic States. They also spent some winters in Maui, Hawaii, and Mesa, Arizona, and took their last cruise through the Panama Canal in 2017.

Robert lived a long life, full of many joyful moments and sad times too. He was a great listener, always wanting to help people, especially to help his children, but many others as well. He loved to laugh, to tell stories, to talk and joke around with one and all. He made a deliberate and long-standing effort always to tell a caller or visitor how wonderful it was to hear from them, to thank them for taking the time to see him. We take from this habit a desire always to make others know how much he appreciated them as human beings. He endured the death of his true love, Loretta, in 2020, and the death of two of his sons, Dan and Phil, in July 2021. He loved to perform and listen to sacred choral music, especially Handel's Messiah, and Bach's Cantatas. Robert lived a long, modest, and very human life. He will be missed by all who knew him.
